Case study 02

Simplifying optimization without hiding the business logic.

The product needed to make cross-channel growth feel manageable while preserving the expert controls advertisers rely on to trust performance decisions.

Product judgment
The design reduced workflow complexity while preserving the business logic advertisers needed to understand optimization tradeoffs.
Monetization relevance
The work connected advertiser value, campaign setup, optimization review, and measurable revenue context in a 0→1 platform product.
Scale relevance
The system had to operate across fragmented objectives, channels, budgets, product surfaces, and cross-functional platform constraints.
Problem
Advertisers needed simpler optimization across fragmented channels, budgets, objectives, and campaign states.
Role
Led 0-1 product design strategy, workflow architecture, and cross-functional alignment with product, engineering, and data partners.
Design challenge
Simplify campaign decisions without hiding the business logic advertisers needed to understand and trust.
Process
Structured the decision hierarchy, mapped adoption paths, and translated complex optimization logic into progressive product surfaces.
Solution
Created a streamlined flow for setup, review, and optimization across channels, reducing friction while preserving expert control.
Impact
$1.3B ARR product context, 78% retention, and 30% adopter share-shift spend.
Public context
Public reporting describes Meta omnichannel ads as connecting online discovery with nearby product availability, store visits, and promotions; early pilot reporting cited 15% lower CPA, 12% higher ROAS, and 3.5x higher purchase conversion than purchase-only optimization. Source
